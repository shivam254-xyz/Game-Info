About the Game

Detroit: Become Human is a 2018 adventure game developed by Quantic Dream and published by Sony Interactive Entertainment. The game is set in a futuristic Detroit where highly advanced androids designed to serve humans begin to develop sentience. Players control three playable androids — Kara, Connor, and Markus — whose stories explore freedom, identity, and the value of life.

The gameplay focuses on narrative and player choice, with decisions affecting the story’s outcome and character relationships. Each character’s journey offers a unique perspective: Kara’s struggle to protect a young girl, Connor’s role as an investigator hunting rogue androids, and Markus’s leadership in seeking freedom for sentient machines. The game’s plot branches based on choices, making player decisions central to the experience.

Detroit: Become Human is heavily focused on player choice and consequence. Almost every decision affects the storyline, character relationships, and even who survives. The game features multiple branching paths and endings, making each playthrough unique. A flowchart system shows how decisions influence the story, encouraging replayability.

The game follows three main android characters: Kara, Connor, and Markus. Each character represents a different perspective on android existence. Kara focuses on themes of empathy and protection, Connor explores duty and logic as an android investigator, and Markus represents rebellion and the fight for freedom. Their stories intersect and evolve based on the choices made by the player.

Detroit: Become Human is praised for its cinematic presentation, emotional storytelling, and detailed graphics. The motion capture performances and impactful narrative choices give the game a deep interactive drama feel, with each playthrough potentially leading to different endings. The themes of autonomy and humanity make it one of the most memorable narrative games of its generation.
