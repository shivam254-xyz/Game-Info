Resident Evil

Quick Info

Genre: Survival Horror

Developer: Capcom

First Release: 1996

Platforms: PC, PlayStation, Xbox

About the Game

Resident Evil is a legendary survival horror game series known for its intense atmosphere, limited resources, and deep storytelling. Players explore dangerous environments filled with zombies and biological threats while uncovering dark secrets.

Resident Evil is one of the most influential survival horror game franchises in gaming history. Developed by Capcom, the series introduced players to a world filled with biological experiments, zombies, and terrifying creatures created by deadly viruses. The games focus heavily on atmosphere, tension, and limited resources, forcing players to think carefully before every action.

A key feature of Resident Evil is its emphasis on survival rather than action. Players must manage ammunition, health items, and save points while exploring dark and dangerous environments such as abandoned mansions, ruined cities, and secret laboratories. This creates constant fear and suspense, which defines the franchise.

The series is also known for its strong storytelling. Resident Evil explores themes like corporate greed, unethical scientific experiments, and the consequences of human ambition. Characters such as Leon S. Kennedy, Jill Valentine, and Chris Redfield have become iconic due to their deep development across multiple games.

Over time, Resident Evil has evolved by introducing new gameplay styles, including action-oriented combat and first-person horror, while still maintaining its core identity. Modern remakes and new releases have helped the franchise remain relevant, making it a perfect example of how classic games can adapt to modern gaming standards.
